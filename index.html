About Me

I’m a final-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign, specializing in AI-driven optimization with real-world impact in operations research — including routing, scheduling, and resource allocation in multi-agent systems. My research, supported by NASA, has been presented to industry leaders and published in top AI and robotics venues, with my latest work submitted to AAAI and ICLR.

I bring a uniquely diverse technical foundation — advanced machine learning (GNNs, attention architectures), robust programming skills (Python, MATLAB, C), and deep hardware experience (FPGA, robotics, mechatronics). I’ve applied these skills in high-stakes, industry settings, including developing an AI architecture for Goodyear Intelligent Mobility Solutions to power smart tires capable of predicting road conditions.

Beyond the technical, I thrive in leadership and cross-disciplinary collaboration. I’ve led multi-institutional projects bridging engineers, doctors, and hospitals, served as a symposium referee, taught university-level courses, and held executive roles in academic events. My work is fueled by a passion for problem-solving, communication, and impact — whether that’s in a lab, a boardroom, or a client strategy session.

Driven by curiosity and challenge, I’m passionate about management, strategy, and innovation — applying rigorous analysis to solve complex problems and deliver meaningful results.
